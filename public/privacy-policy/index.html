Data Collection

This website collects a small amount of information about visits for analytics and troubleshooting purposes. The information collected is limited to the following:

What We Store

  • The pathname of your visit.
    This is used to measure page views and understand which pages are visited most frequently.
  • The country associated with your visit.
    This is used to understand the geographic distribution of visitors and identify which regions are using the website.
  • The timestamp of your visit.
    This is used to determine when visits occurred and to assist with troubleshooting issues that may be related to specific website versions or deployments.
  • The referrer.
    This indicates which page or website led you to this website. It is used to understand how visitors discover the website and which external sources are directing traffic to it.
  • The user-agent of your visit.
    This is used to determine whether the request was authentic or not.
  • The visitor ID.
    The visitor ID is a random anonymous identifier. This is used to distinguish unique visitors, and provide support for data deletion. We do not collect real IP addresses. The visitor ID is not linked to any accounts or a personal identity. Users can clear it by clearing site data / localStorage. We strongly recommend not touching the visitor ID, though; server-based features will stop working unless you know what you're doing.

    Your visitor ID is .... If you don't see a visitor ID, that means you do not have data collection enabled, and the site hasn't generated a user ID for you yet. Alternatively, you can check for the visitorId field in your browser's localStorage.

No advertising cookies are used, and no user accounts are required to access the website. The collected data is not sold, shared, or used for advertising purposes.

This website's analytics system is open source. You may review the implementation in the source code.

How to Opt Out

On your first visit, an information panel will appear. Analytics collection is disabled by default and will remain disabled until you make a choice. If you choose to enable analytics, anonymous visit information will be collected according to this privacy policy. If you choose to disable analytics, no analytics data will be collected. Once you have made a choice, the panel will not appear again unless you reset your preference.

You can change your preference at any time using the button below. Your current choice is stored locally in your browser and can be updated whenever you wish.

Note: This preference is stored in your browser's localStorage. Clearing your browser data, using a different browser, or using a different device may reset this setting.

How to Request Data Deletion

If you would like data associated with your visits to be removed, feel free to get in touch and provide information such as the visitor ID, your country, your browser, and more.
