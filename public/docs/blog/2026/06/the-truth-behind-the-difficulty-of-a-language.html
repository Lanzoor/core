The Truth Behind the Difficulty of a Language

Here's a simple question. What is the hardest language to learn? One could say the answer to this question is Arabic, Chinese, Japanese, or something entirely else. This question has (probably) existed ever since languages were a thing, and while I do think it's a very interesting question, I believe that arguing about the difficulty of a language is utterly stupid and unnecessary. Seriously, you shouldn't waste time trying to come up with an answer to this question, as it's simply a waste of time. So without further ado, let's jump right into my (probably disputable) opinion.

The difficulty of a language is entirely subjective and relative. Let's start with the subjective aspect of a language. A language has multiple factors that determine the overall difficulty, such as the sentence structure, the phonetic consistency, and the case system.
