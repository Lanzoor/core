Monthly Recap | June 2026

Hello! It's already been a month, huh?
A lot of things happened in June, so I've gathered up all of the important details and summarized 'em.
So without further ado, let's begin!

Navigation UI/UX Overhaul

The long-awaited Navigation UI/UX overhaul has been finally released! For full details, check out the changelog. We believe this update marks a strong step toward improving the overall user experience.

That said, since this update is rather experimental, we'd like to hear more from you! Feel free to get in touch for any feedback or questions.

As you can probably see, the old one was pretty much unmaintained for months, and also had an outdated font. The new overhaul fixed this by applying the correct font, improving the styles, and even improving compatiability for mobile devices.

A Major Change for core

After some consideration, we've decided to migrate the entire project from our outdated custom routing system to SvelteKit!

Why?

Why the sudden switch, you may ask? Good question! As the site continued to grow, maintaining the codebase became increasingly challenging. Our previous custom Vite-based setup had become outdated and difficult to work with.
This is when one of my team members recommended SvelteKit. After trying it out, I realized it was a much better fit for what I wanted to achieve with the project.

What should I expect from the transition?

Unfortunately, since this is a significant transition, we won't be making any new changes to the existing project until the migration is complete. This will likely be the last version we release for a while. We will also not be collecting any analytics during the transition.

We appreciate your understanding in the meantime.

Will the website change in any way?

If you're asking about the public-facing structure (e.g. styles, structure, accessibility, features, API, etc.), then nope! The new version will be thoroughly reviewed before release, so you won't have to worry about a thing.

However, the codebase will be significantly revamped, so team members are expected to adapt to the new structure.
